Continents

A continent is any of several large landmasses. Generally identified by convention rather than any strict criteria, up to seven geographical regions are commonly regarded as continents.

Continents are usually seperated by huge bodies of water or oceans.

Earth had 7 continents: Asia, Australia, Africa, Antartica, Europe, North America, South America. Asia is known for being the largest continent in both size and population while Australia is the smallest in size and Antartica is the smallest in population.

A major part of the land of continents lies in the Northern Hemisphere while the continents Australia and Antartica completely in the Southern Hemisphere.

About 200 million years ago, all the continents on Earth were actually one huge "supercontinent" surrounded by one enormous ocean. This gigantic continent, called Pangaea , slowly broke apart and spread out to form the continents we know today.
