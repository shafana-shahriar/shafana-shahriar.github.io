The Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only astronomical object known to harbor life. While large volumes of water can be found throughout the Solar System, only Earth sustains liquid surface water. About 71% of Earth's surface is made up of the ocean, dwarfing Earth's polar ice, lakes, and rivers.

The Earth is over 4.5 billion years old and has a radius of 6,371 km. Its distance from the sun is 149.6 million km. The surface area of the Earth is 510.1 million km² and the land area is 148.9 million km². The population is over 7.7 billion

The Solar System

The Solar System is the gravitationally bound system of the Sun and the objects that orbit it.It formed 4.6 billion years ago from the gravitational collapse of a giant interstellar molecular cloud. The vast majority of the system's mass is in the Sun, with most of the remaining mass contained in the planet Jupiter.

Our Solar System is 4.571 billion years old and consists of 1,199,224 (discovered)minor planets, 9 known dwarf planets, 1 star (the Sun) and has 657 natural satellites, 206 which are planetary and 451 which are mior planetary.

The Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of the Solar System. It is a nearly perfect ball of hot plasma, heated to incandescence by nuclear fusion reactions in its core, radiating the energy mainly as light, ultraviolet, and infrared radiation. It is the most important source of energy for life on Earth.The Sun is 4.603 billion years old and in 149.6 million kilmetres away from planet Earth. Its mass is 1.989 × 10^30 kg and its radius in 696,340 km. The Sun has a surface temperature of 5,778 K and has 2 moons (3122 Florence and 90482 Orcus)

The Moon

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite. Together with Earth it forms the Earth-Moon satellite system. It is about one-quarter of Earth in diameter. In the Solar System it is the fifth largest satellite, larger than any of the known dwarf planets and the largest satellite of a planet relative to the planet. The Moon is 384,000 kilometres away from Earth and has the gravity of 1.62 m/s². Its surface area is 3.793×10⁷ km² and the surface pressure of the Moon is 10−⁷ Pa (1 picobar) (day); 10−¹⁰ Pa (1 femtobar); (night)

Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in the Solar System and the closest to the Sun. Its orbit around the Sun takes 87.97 Earth days, the shortest of all the Sun's planets. Mercury's distance from the sun is 58 million km. It has a surface area of 74.8 million km², a radius of 2,439.7 km, and a mass of 3.285 × 10^23 kg (0.055 M⊕). Its density is 5.43 g/cm³ and the length of day is 58d 15h and 30m.

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty. Venus can cast shadows and can be visible to the naked eye in broad daylight. Venus's distance from the sun is 108.2 million km and its orbital period is 225 days. Venus's mass is 4.867 × 10^24 kg (0.815 M⊕) and its radius is 6,051.8 km. The length of day on Venus is 116d 18 and 0m.

Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and the second-smallest planet in the Solar System, being larger than only Mercury. In the English language, Mars is named for the Roman god of war. Mars' distancefrom the Sun is 227.9 million km. It has the surface area of 144.8 million km², gravity of 3.721 m/s² and mass of 6.39 × 10^23 kg (0.107 M⊕). It has 2 moons( Phobos and Delmos) and the length of day is 1d 0h 37m.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in the Solar System. It is a gas giant with a mass more than two and a half times that of all the other planets in the Solar System combined, but slightly less than one-thousandth the mass of the Sun.Jupiter has a mass of 1.898 × 10^27 kg (317.8 M⊕ and gravity of 24.79 m/s². The length of day in Jupiter is 0d 9h and 56m and its distance from the SUn is 778.5 million km.

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest in the Solar System, after Jupiter. It is a gas giant with an average radius of about nine and a half times that of Earth. It has only one-eighth the average density of Earth; however, with its larger volume, Saturn is over 95 times more massive. Saturn's distance from the Sun is 1.434 billion kilometers. It has a radius of 58,232 km and mass of 5.683 × 10^26 kg (95.16 M⊕).

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun. Its name is a reference to the Greek god of the sky, Uranus, who, according to Greek mythology, was the great-grandfather of Ares, grandfather of Zeus and father of Cronus. It has the third-largest planetary radius and fourth-largest planetary mass in the Solar System. Uranus' distance from the Sun is 2.871 billion km. Its surface area is 8.083 billion km² and its radius is 25,362 km. Uranus' orbital period is 84 years.

Neptune

Neptune is the eighth planet from the Sun and the farthest known solar planet. In the Solar System, it is the fourth-largest planet by diameter, the third-most-massive planet, and the densest giant planet. It is 17 times the mass of Earth, and slightly more massive than its near-twin Uranus. Neptune's distnce from the Sun is 4.495 billion km. It has a mass of 1.024 × 10^26 kg (17.15 M⊕), surface area of 7.618 billion km² and radius of 24,622 km. Neptune's orbital period is 165 years.
