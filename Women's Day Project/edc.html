Empress Dowager Cixi

Empress Dowager Cixi, of the Manchu Yehe Nara clan, was a Chinese noblewoman, concubine and later regent who effectively controlled the Chinese government in the late Qing dynasty for 47 years, from 1861 until her death in 1908. She was married to Xianfeng Emperor with whom she had two children, Tongzhi Emperor and Princess Rongshu. She is buried in Qing Dong Ling, Tangshan, China.

Although she had ruled China for nearly 50 years, many textbooks blame her unchecked thievery for the nation's greatest failures but historians increasingly say that she was a strong leader with a gangster like cunning mindset.

Eventhough Cixi hadn't been the best Empress, she made some modernizing accomplishments that include banning foot-binding, reforming the legal code and the education system, and outlawing certain barbaric punishments.Cixi had also sought to transform China into a constitunional monarchy which had cut short by her death.

Cixi was also known as the Dragon Lady because of her cunning, manipulative and spitefullness even thought her it quickened the end of the Qing Dynasty.
