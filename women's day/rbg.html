Ruth Bader Ginsburg

Joan Ruth Bader Ginsburg was an American lawyer and jurist who served as an associate justice of the Supreme Court of the United States from 1993 until her death in 2020.Ruth had graduated first in her class from Columbia Law School and personally battled-and overcomed-sexism.

Ruth was the first ever person on both the Harvard and Columbia law reviews and became the second female law professor at Rutgers where she also fought for equal pay.

Ginsburg was the first Jewish woman (yes, she was Jewish) and the second women to serve on the Court , the first being Sandra Day O'Connor.She was nominated by Bill Clinton, preceded by Byron White and succeded by Amy Coney Barrett
